Zone 1 • 0m

Intertidal Zone (The Coast)

The dynamic ecotone between high and low tide.

Animals here live a bipolar life: drowning in crashing waves at high tide, and baking in the hot sun at low tide.

SciOly Pro-Tip

Organisms exposed to air face severe desiccation. Mussels excrete byssal threads. They use Circadian-Tidal rhythms for spawning.

Zone 2 • Continental Shelf

Neritic Zone (Coastal Seas)

The shallow, sunlit waters over the continental shelf.

Rich in nutrients from land runoff and upwelling, this zone supports the highest biodiversity in the ocean, including coral reefs and kelp forests.

SciOly Pro-Tip

High productivity is driven by Upwelling and land runoff. Corals have a mutualistic relationship with photosynthetic Zooxanthellae.

Zone 3 • 0 - 200m

Epipelagic Zone (Sunlight Zone)

The bright, uppermost layer.

The biological engine of the ocean! Sunlight penetrates here allowing photosynthesis.

SciOly Pro-Tip

Red light absorbs first. Predators use Fusiform morphology. Bony fish use Physostomous or Physoclistous swim bladders.

Zone 4 • 200 - 1,000m

Mesopelagic Zone (Twilight Zone)

The dimly lit middle ocean.

Only faint blue light makes it here. Animals use large eyes and counter-illumination.

SciOly Pro-Tip

Contains the Oxygen Minimum Zone (OMZ) where bacterial respiration of marine snow depletes oxygen.

Zone 5 • 1,000 - 4,000m

Bathypelagic & Bathyal (Midnight Zone)

Total darkness, crushing pressure, and hydrothermal vents.

The Bathypelagic is the open water where food mostly comes from falling Marine Snow. The Bathyal zone is the seafloor beneath it, home to benthic scavengers and hydrothermal vent communities.

SciOly Pro-Tip

Bioluminescence relies on Luciferin and Luciferase. In the 1870s, the Challenger Expedition disproved the Azoic Hypothesis (theory of no deep life).

Zone 6 • 4,000 - 6,000m

Abyssopelagic & Abyssal (The Abyss)

The vast, muddy, featureless plains.

The pressure here is crushing! While the Abyssopelagic water column is sparse, most life here is benthic (living on the seafloor mud).

SciOly Pro-Tip

Organisms living in mud are Benthic. The Abyss relies entirely on external energy (marine snow), making it an Allochthonous ecosystem. Hydrostatic pressure increases 1 atm every 10m.

Zone 7 • 6,000 - 11,000m

Hadalpelagic & Hadal Benthic (The Trenches)

Deepest scars in the earth's crust.

Conditions are so extreme that standard proteins naturally unravel! Most life is concentrated on the trench floor (benthic).

SciOly Pro-Tip

Vents produce local energy (Autochthonous). Bacteria here are Hyperthermophiles (>100°C). TMAO piezolytes prevent protein crushing. Cold Seeps use Methane (CH4).
