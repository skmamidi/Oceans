Thermocline

A zone of rapid temperature decrease. Cold water is denser, so it sinks below the warm surface water, creating a barrier.

Interactive Sensor: Temperature Drop

Halocline

A zone of rapid change in salinity (saltiness). High evaporation at the surface or ice melting can create dense, salty layers that act like liquid floors.

Oxycline

A rapid drop in dissolved oxygen, creating an Oxygen Minimum Zone where only specialized creatures survive.

Nutricline

A rapid increase in nutrients (nitrates/phosphates) as dead organic matter sinks and decays in the deep.

Deep Scattering Layer (DSL)

A dense layer of organisms that migrate vertically. It's so thick it bounces back sonar pings, creating a "false bottom" on ship radars.

Chemocline

A strong chemical boundary separating distinct water masses, often featuring lack of oxygen and high hydrogen sulfide. Some rare ecosystems build entirely around these toxic chemicals!

Thermohaline Circulation

The global ocean conveyor belt — cold, dense, salty water drives currents spanning all oceans.

Interactive Model: Global Conveyor Belt

🌡️ Warm salty water flows north, cools and SINKS. Cold dense water flows back south along the deep ocean floor. One complete circuit takes ~1,000 years and regulates Earth's entire climate!
